The First Network for Research


Advance Research Project Agency Network began development in 1966 by the US ARPA.It is a wide area network linking many universities and research centers. It was first to use packet switching and Donald Davies coined the term packets.The first two nodes came to existence was established between UCLA and Stanford Research Institute (SRI) in 1969. The next nodes where established between UCSB and University of Utah. ARPANET was about time-sharing. The research institutions use the processing power of the other research institution during large calculation that requires more power.
